# GAYA — Complete Game Flow
> **Theme:** "We don’t see things as they are… only as our hearts allow."

---

## PROLOGUE — THE BEDROOM
Bedroom 001
**Scene:** Darkness. A single lamp. A pink diary sits open on a desk; one page is torn at the edge. 
**Transition:** Fade to white, then into full morning light.
**Setting:** Gaya’s bedroom. Outside, the sounds of a neighborhood (jeepneys, birds). On the wall, a countdown strip ends at “0” circled in pink.

Bedroom 002-004
*   **Character Action:** Gaya wakes up, sees the countdown, and jumps with excitement. "Eighteen. Today. It’s actually today."


Bedroom 004
### [PLAYER INTERACTION: Pick up torn paper]
*   **Discovery:** She finds a torn piece of paper on the nightstand.
**Overlay:** Gaya’s handwriting (rushed).
> "Reminder for the big day —
> **Living room:** the three black vases. The one with the dancing figures is still in pieces in the lower cabinet. Put it together first. Then the flowers — the basket will show you which flower belongs to which vase. Trust the shadow.
> **Bedroom:** The gown is on the bed. The mirror will show you exactly what you’re supposed to look like. Match it.
> **Kitchen:** 18 candles on the table. Three aren’t placed right yet — fix the order, light them left to right.

## LEVEL 1 — THE LIVING ROOM
**Scene:** The *sala*. Morning light. Two black vases are on a shelf; a third space is empty.

### GAMEPLAY STEP 1 — ASSEMBLE THE VASE
*   **Task:** Open the lower cabinet to find three ceramic fragments.
*   **Puzzle:** Drag-and-drop the pieces until they click together. 
*   **Result:** The vase (depicting a dancing figure) is placed on the shelf.
*   **Lore:** Gaya recalls her Lola saying, *"You’ll understand the symbols when you need to."*

### GAMEPLAY STEP 2 — GET THE BASKET
*   **Task:** Interact with the wicker basket in the corner.
*   **Note Found:**
    *   Sampaguita → dancing figure
    *   Roses → arms spread wide
    *   Lilies → looking up
    *   *Watch the shadow. It won’t lie to you.*

### GAMEPLAY STEP 3 — SHADOW MATCHING
*   **Mechanic:** Each flower casts a unique shadow.
    *   **Sampaguita:** Small tight cluster.
    *   **Roses:** Wide, overlapping arcs.
    *   **Lilies:** Tall and narrow, reaching upward.
*   **Interaction:** Match the shadow to the corresponding symbol on the vase. A soft gold glow confirms a correct match.

**Narrative Beat:** Mother enters, inspects the repaired vase, touches a sampaguita petal, and leaves silently. Gaya realizes: *"She loves it. She won’t say so... but she touched it."*

### DIARY ENTRY 1
**Location:** Under the sampaguita vase.
> "March 3: I asked Mommy what her favorite flower was... She said sampaguita because it’s small, but you can smell it from across the room... I want to mean more than I look."

---

## LEVEL 2 — THE BEDROOM
**Scene:** Mid-morning. A white gown is on the bed. A full-length mirror stands in the center, showing a blurred "ghost" reflection of Gaya.

### THE GHOST MIRROR PUZZLE
**Goal:** Match Gaya’s physical appearance to the solid "ghost" image in the mirror.

| Choice | Correct Option | Gaya's Thought |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Pin** | Small sampaguita pin | "Fill the room without announcing yourself." |
| **The Hem** | Floor-length (feet covered) | "Like I have nowhere to rush to." |
| **The Hair** | Fully down | "Put-together isn’t what tonight is about." |
| **The Sleeves** | Down to the wrist | "The ghost was always showing the truth." |

**Narrative Beat:** Once matched, Gaya and the reflection are identical. When she walks away, the reflection remains still for three seconds before fading like breath on glass.

### DIARY ENTRY 2
**Location:** Glowing on the bed.
> "October 14: ...Mommy walked into the room... She looked at me for a second and said — why do you look like you’re apologizing? ...What would it feel like to just look like myself and not want to apologize for it?"

---

## LEVEL 3 — THE KITCHEN
**Scene:** 5:00 PM gold light. 18 candle holders on a round table. 15 are lit; 3 are misplaced and unlit.

### GAMEPLAY — THE CANDLE SWAP
*   **Puzzle:** Reference a chart on the counter to place the three mismatched candles in the correct circular order (names/positions).
*   **The Struggle:** The final candle (Position 18) will not light. Matches keep going out.

### DIARY ENTRY 3
**Location:** Under the 18th candle holder.
> "May 12: I’ve been trying to write something for Mommy all week... It’s more like — you saw me when I couldn’t see myself... This last candle is hers. Only she—" *(The paper is torn)*.

**Narrative Beat:** Mother enters. She takes the matches and lights the 18th candle on the first strike. They stand in the amber glow together.

### THE REVEAL (FADE TO BLACK)
The text of the diary entry completes on screen:
> "...Only she can light it. And if you’re reading this, Mommy — it means you’ve seen everything I left for you... Thank you for knowing my whole name when I only knew half of it. **Mahal na mahal kita. — Ligaya**"

---

## EPILOGUE — THE MOTHER
**Scene:** Next morning. Player controls the Mother.
*   **Action:** Walk from the kitchen to Gaya’s bedroom.
*   **Interaction:** Pick up the pink diary on the bed. 

### THE FINAL MECHANIC
The camera zooms into the diary.
*   **Text:** "Gaya Maria Reyes | May 13 — 18th Birthday | We don’t see things as they are"
*   **Player Action:** Use the pen to add "LI" to "Gaya" and complete the sentence.
*   **Result:** 
    > **LIGAYA**
    > "We don’t see things as they are… **only as our hearts allow.**"

## ENDINGS & CREDITS

*   **Post-Credits:** The camera reveals a banner previously hidden by a curtain: **HAPPY BIRTHDAY, LIGAYA**.

## THEMATIC SUMMARY

| Level | Action | Theme |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Living Room** | Match shadow to symbol | The shadow reveals what the object itself cannot show. |
| **Bedroom** | Match the ghost image | Reality is always there; we just have to catch up to it. |
| **Kitchen** | Lighting the 18th candle | Some truths cannot be completed alone. |
| **Epilogue** | Completing the name | The game's theme becomes the final mechanic. |
